# Website institutional and investor-readiness review

Date: 2026-07-20

## Review standard

This review asks whether the public site can survive a skeptical technical scan, communicate a new infrastructure category in one screen, and give partners or investors a credible path from positioning to inspectable evidence. It does **not** treat attractive design as proof of adoption or investment readiness.

## What a skeptical evaluator can now verify

1. Vault is positioned as a governance foundation, not merely another vector memory engine.
2. The published mem0 comparison holds the engine and candidate pool constant and measures the governance lift.
3. The 66.7% to 100% Valid Recall@1 movement comes from six disclosed synthetic top-1 cases.
4. The 33.3% to 0% forbidden-exposure movement is attributable to the temporal-correction and superseded-revision cases.
5. The mean paired guard overhead is +0.1482 ms under this frozen harness.
6. AgentMemory remains diagnostic and Letta / MemGPT remains unmeasured; neither is presented as a published win.
7. A standalone verifier checks all 36 bundle files, checksum coverage, safe paths, artifact-index structure, and publishable track declarations without requiring the website or provider runtime.

## Evidence still required before an institutional fundraising claim

- Independent provider rerun of the published experiment; artifact verification alone is not reproduction.
- Published A versus A + Vault tracks for at least two more memory engines.
- End-to-end answer-quality evaluation on an established long-term-memory benchmark.
- Real workload scale tests, concurrent writers, large corpora, and failure recovery.
- Adoption evidence: active installations, retained projects, governed memory volume, and integration growth.
- Customer or design-partner references with permission to publish.
- A clear commercial model, security posture, roadmap, and team narrative.

## Traffic reality

A website can improve conversion and shareability; it cannot guarantee one million visits. Reaching that scale requires a repeatable distribution system: benchmark releases worth citing, integration pages, technical essays, launch moments, community examples, search instrumentation, and earned links. Traffic must be measured as acquisition, engaged reading, repository conversion, installation, and retained usage—not pageviews alone.
